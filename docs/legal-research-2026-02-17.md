# buildit legal research notes (as of 2026-02-17)

## Korea

1) FAR upper bounds by zoning
- Source: National Law Information Center, Enforcement Decree of National Land Planning and Utilization Act, Article 85
- URL: https://www.law.go.kr/lsLawLinkInfo.do?lsJoLnkSeq=1000616250&chrClsCd=010202
- Key point: Article 85 sets zoning-specific FAR ranges and delegates exact local values to municipal ordinance.

2) Sunlight / height control
- Source: National Law Information Center, Building Act, Article 61
- URL: https://www.law.go.kr/lsLawLinkInfo.do?chrClsCd=010202&lsJoLnkSeq=1000990550
- Key point: In dedicated/general residential areas, height is limited based on north-side boundary distance for daylight.

3) Local daylight setback example (Seoul)
- Source: Seoul Metropolitan City Building Ordinance, Article 35
- URL: https://www.law.go.kr/LSW/ordinSideInfoP.do?chrClsCd=010202&docCls=jo&gubun=&joBrNo=00&joNo=0035&ordinSeq=2054495&urlMode=ordinScJoAllRltInfoR
- Key point: Explicit setback distances/ratios are enforced (e.g., 10m threshold + 1/2 height rule for upper parts).

4) Qualitative district controls
- Source: Enforcement Decree of National Land Planning and Utilization Act, Article 72
- URL: https://www.law.go.kr/lsLinkCommonInfo.do?lsJoLnkSeq=1026833171&chrClsCd=&ancYnChk=
- Key point: In landscape districts, limits can include height, maximum width, color, and landscaping by ordinance.

5) Committee-based qualitative review (Seoul)
- Source: Seoul Housing/Architecture page for architectural committee review
- URL: https://news.seoul.go.kr/citybuild/archives/139
- Key point: Seoul publishes official review standards and operating rules (latest listed updates include 2025-10-10 and 2025-11-27 entries).

6) Household size trend (demand-side reason for unit sizing)
- Source: Statistics Korea press release, Household Projections
- URL: https://kostat.go.kr/board.es?act=view&bid=204&list_no=430830&mid=a10301010000&nPage=1
- Key point: 1-person households continue to increase and average household size trends downward, supporting compact/mid-size unit mix assumptions.

7) Socially recognized housing size threshold
- Source: Housing Act Article 2 (National Housing Size concept)
- URL: https://www.law.go.kr/법령/주택법/제2조
- Key point: The 85㎡ threshold is widely used in policy/market segmentation and is relevant when defining mainstream apartment product mix.

## International references for expansion

1) Singapore (URA)
- URL: https://www.ura.gov.sg/Corporate/Guidelines/Development-Control/Residential/Flats-Condominiums/Gross-Plot-Ratio
- Key point: Master Plan GPR is upper bound; actual achievable GPR depends on setbacks, height and technical constraints.

2) New York City
- URL: https://zoningresolution.planning.nyc.gov/index.php/article-ii/chapter-4/24-11
- Key point: Zoning Resolution explicitly couples max FAR with lot coverage and bulk controls.
